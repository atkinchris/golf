# Dice Golf

Roll your d6 to determine how far you'll hit the ball.

Add 1 to your roll if you are hitting from the fairway.
Subtract 1 from your roll if you're hitting from a sand trap.
The effective roll is always at least 1 (you can never roll zero movement).

Pick a direction for your hit. You may move in one of the 8 orthogonal or diagonal directions.

Check your landing spot and make sure your path is clear. You may only go through trees if you are hitting from the fairway (see Terrain section for more info).

## Re-rolling

When teeing off, if you don't like your roll, you may re-roll once. You must use the second roll amount. The same goes for Mulligans.

You have 6 "Mulligans" per hole that can be used to re-roll any time.

## Putting

You may always choose to putt (move 1 space) on your turn. You may even roll the die and instead choose to putt instead of move the indicated number of spaces.

### Overshooting the hole

If you cross over the hole in the course of your movement path, the ball doesn't necessarily "go in" the cup. However, you may overshoot the hole by 1 space in this case and count the ball as going in. Otherwise, the ball goes "over" the hole.

## Scoring

Count your strokes. The par on each hole is 6. Sometimes you'll go over, sometimes you'll go under.

## Terrain

### Rough

This makes up the majority of the terrain on each hole. You move the exact amount you roll. You may also putt in the rough.

### Fairway

It's good to try to stay on the fairway. This gives +1 to your roll. You can hit the ball over trees only when hitting from the fairway. You may also putt on the fairway. The green (area around the hole) counts as fairway.

### Sand trap

Sand traps lessen your ability to move. Subtract 1 from your roll. You may also putt to move 1 space in sand.

### Water hazard

You may not land in water. Your hit may travel over water though.

### Trees

You may not land on trees. Your hit may not travel over trees, except when hitting from the fairway.

### Slopes

Arrows indicate a slope. If you land on one, your ball rolls 1 space in that direction.

Notes on slopes:

- If the new space is also a slope, your ball keeps rolling until it comes to a space without one.
- If a slope points towards water, ignore the slope.
- If two slopes point directly at one another, stop the ball after the first slope movement.
